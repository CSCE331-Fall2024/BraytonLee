Portfolio

In addition to the standard projects that accompanied my degree, I have built several successful systems throughout my career that have greatly enhance productivity, reduced overhead costs, and improved safety.

My first major project involved designing and implementing a frictionless process for machinists to track production, enabling management to identify areas of improvement in several key areas.

In 2014, my wife and I moved to Colorado, where I worked at Colorado State University. There I led two major projects. First, I designed and automated a time-tracking process for employees, reducing the required effort to produce, inspect, and log entries by more than 95%. I also developed a user-friendly, easy-to-maintain database and process for tracking inventory and capital assets.

Most recently, I modeled and prototyped a low-cost mesh network of air-monitoring sensors for first-responder HAZMAT teams. The system deploys small edge sensors in hot and cold zones to monitor conditions without risking first responders during emergencies.

In the near future, I am working towards two major goals. First, I will build a machine vision and machine learning system to supplement health and environmental monitoring for animals housed in fixed facilities, for example animals in shelters, hospitals, and research. The purpose of this system is to provide a second line of defense in identifying illness or abnormal conditions in a constantly monitored system, and to assist every hard working and well-meaning care giver with minimal cost

Additionally, it is also my most heartfelt desire to work with others in advancing the cause of science and spaceflight by working with NASA or another space-focused organization. Most people think of “space exploration” as simply looking up; however, nearly every discovery and invention from space research and engineering has the potential to directly impact live on Earth, making it a better and safer for everyone.
