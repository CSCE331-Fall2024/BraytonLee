Portfolio

Throughout my career, I have built several successful systems that have greatly improved work productivity, reduced overhead costs, and improved safety.

My first major project involved designing and implementing a frictionless process for machinists to track their production, enabling management to identify areas of improvement in several key areas.

In 2014, my wife and I moved to Colorado, where I worked at Colorado State University. While there I led and worked on two major projects. First, I designed and automated a time-tracking process for employees that reducing the required effort to produce, inspect, and log entries by more than 95%.

I also developed a database and process for tracking inventory and capital assets that was both user-friendly and easy to maintain.

Most recently, I modeled and prototyped a mesh network of air monitoring sensors that can be used by a fire department HAZMAT team. The system consists of small edge sensors that are to be distributed within hot and cold zones to monitor changing conditions without risking first responders during an emergency response.

In the near future, I am working towards two major goals. First, I will build a machine vision and machine learning system to supplement health and environmental monitoring for animals housed in fixed facilities, for example animals in shelters, hospitals, and research. The purpose of this system is to provide a second line of defense in identifying illness or abnormal conditions in a constantly monitored system, and to assist every hard working and well-meaning care giver with minimal cost

Additionally, it is also my most heartfelt desire to work with others in advancing the cause of science and spaceflight by working with NASA or another space-focused organization. Most people think of “space exploration” as simply looking up; however, nearly every discovery and invention from space research and engineering has the potential to directly impact live on Earth, making it a better and safer for everyone.
