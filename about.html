A Brief Introduction

I live with my wife and our small menagerie of pets, and we both serve as volunteer firefighters in our community. In my free time, I enjoy two contrasting sports: powerlifting and ultra-endurance cycling. My current long-term goal is to complete two different cycling events: Paris-Brest-Paris and London-Edinburgh-London. Both are rides of about 750 miles that must be completed in less than 90 hours. My wife and I have also been playing TTRPGs with a small group of close friends since 2015.

Academically, I have a diverse set of interests; however, my primary interest is in using automation to make our lives safer. Humans can make amazing leaps in reasoning to achieve discoveries. We also quickly become task -saturated, confused, or just plain bored, leading to mistakes. Machine tools and intelligence can supplement our abilities preventing errors, or to see through noise to find patterns in tremendous amounts of data.

As technology continues to permeate every aspect of our lives, we have an opportunity to prevent accidents, discover illnesses, and intervene to prevent harms that happen daily. The responsible and ethical creation of these technologies are core values that I hold.

With responsible technology creation and use comes the need for strong security behind those systems. I am fascinated by cybersecurity, especially the social engineering aspect of it. Technical exploits are flashy and grab headlines, yet, for the average person, social exploitation is by far the biggest threat. When interacting with the public as a firefighter, I am amazed at how open and honest people can be and how much information people are openly willing to give to strangers. I have learned that education is paramount for everyone involved with technology in any way. From national security to the most vulnerable, appropriate systems must be in place to seamlessly provide peace of mind and the ability to fully and ethically implement these systems into our lives.
